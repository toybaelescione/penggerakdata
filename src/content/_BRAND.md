# Brand Voice — Penggerak Data

## Tagline
*Mulai dari nol. Masuk ke data.*

## Tone of Voice

**Register:** Casual Indonesian. Use "kamu", not "Anda". Write like a senior friend explaining to a junior, not like a textbook.

**Encouragement:** Direct, not hollow.
- ❌ "Kamu pasti bisa! Semangat terus!"
- ✅ "Ini topik yang sering bikin orang stuck — kita bahas pelan-pelan."
- ✅ "Query ini mungkin terlihat rumit, tapi ada pola yang selalu berulang."

**Technical terms:** Use the English term on first mention with Indonesian context in parentheses. Then use the English term going forward — it appears in real job contexts.
- ✅ "window function (fungsi yang bekerja atas sekumpulan baris)" — then just "window function"
- ❌ Always translating: "fungsi jendela" sounds unnatural to practitioners

**Company references:** Always use Indonesian companies as examples. Tokopedia orders, Gojek rides, Tiket.com bookings, Traveloka flights, Bukalapak products.

**What to avoid:**
- Formal Indonesian: *-lah, -kah, demikianlah, dipersilakan*
- Generic Western company examples (Google, Meta, Amazon) where Indonesian examples exist
- Hollow motivational filler
- Overly academic tone

## Color Semantics (for copy decisions)
- Teal (`#4ec9b0`) = completed, success, correct answer
- Orange (`#ce9178`) = important callout, warning, thing to remember
- Yellow (`#dcdcaa`) = section title, key concept name
- Blue (`#007acc`) = interactive, CTA, link
- Comment green (`#6a9955`) = supporting note, "by the way"
